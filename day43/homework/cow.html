Why is cow important?

Cows are important animals because they provide many essential resources that support human life and the economy. They are a primary source of milk, which is used to make dairy products such as cheese, butter, and yogurt, all of which are important for nutrition. Cows also provide meat and leather, contributing to food supply and clothing industries. In agriculture, they help farmers by fertilizing the soil with their manure, which improves crop growth. In some parts of the world, cows are also used for plowing fields and transportation. Overall, cows play a vital role in farming, food production, and sustaining rural communities, making them very important to humans.
